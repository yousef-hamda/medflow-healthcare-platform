# Model Card — Sepsis Early-Warning Score (`sepsis-ews`)

> **SYNTHETIC DATA / NOT FOR CLINICAL USE.** This model is trained and
> evaluated entirely on synthetic patients generated by Synthea (seed 42). It
> is a reference implementation for the MedFlow platform and is **not**
> FDA-cleared, validated on real patients, or suitable for patient care.

## Model details

- **Name / version:** `sepsis-ews`, v0.1.0 (MLflow registry).
- **Type:** Recurrent neural net — 2-layer LSTM (hidden 64, dropout 0.3) with a
  single logit head, trained with `BCEWithLogitsLoss`. See
  `medflow_ml/models/lstm.py`.
- **Owner:** MedFlow ML (demo).
- **Input:** a 6-hour vitals window resampled onto a 15-minute grid
  (24 steps × 5 vitals), z-scored against fixed clinical scales; optional point
  labs (WBC, lactate, creatinine).
- **Output:** per-window sepsis probability `[0, 1]` plus a risk band
  (low `<0.3`, medium `0.3–0.6`, high `≥0.6`) and top-5 SHAP attributions.

## Intended use

- **In scope:** demonstrating a streaming early-warning pipeline on synthetic
  inpatient vitals — feature parity between training and serving, calibration,
  SHAP explanations, drift monitoring and subgroup fairness reporting.
- **Out of scope:** any real patient triage, diagnosis or treatment decision;
  pediatric/neonatal populations; non-inpatient settings; deployment on real
  EHR data without revalidation, prospective study and regulatory clearance.

## Training data

- **Source:** Synthea-generated synthetic encounters (seed 42), loaded into the
  OMOP gold lakehouse. **No real patients, no PHI.**
- **Cohort:** adult inpatient admissions (`adult_inpatient` cohort). Positive
  label = a sepsis condition recorded during the admission (SNOMED concept ids
  91302008, 10001005, 105592009, 434156009, 76571007).
- **Split:** time-aware — admissions ordered by admission date, most recent
  fraction held out for validation (no future-into-past leakage).

## Features

Shared with serving via `medflow_ml.features.vitals` (byte-for-byte): heart
rate, SpO2, respiratory rate, temperature, MAP over the 24×5 grid; LOCF
forward-fill; population-normal imputation for leading gaps. No feature uses
post-prediction-time data (enforced by `test_leakage_guard.py`).

## Failure modes

- **Sparse/late vitals:** leading gaps imputed with population normals can mask
  early deterioration; very short windows are low-confidence.
- **Distribution shift:** synthetic vital dynamics differ from real physiology;
  the model will not transfer to real data without retraining.
- **Class imbalance:** low positive prevalence inflates AUROC relative to AUPRC;
  use AUPRC and operating-point metrics for threshold selection.
- **Label noise:** sepsis labels are condition-record-based, not Sepsis-3
  adjudicated.

## Monitoring plan

- Daily Evidently data-drift report (`jobs/drift_report.py`) comparing recent
  serving inputs to the training baseline; alert on PSI/feature drift.
- Track alert rate, calibration (ECE) and subgroup AUROC on labeled feedback.
- Watch online↔offline feature skew (shared featurizer guards this by design).

## Retraining cadence

Retrain on schedule (e.g. monthly) or on a drift alert / material data-schema
change. Always re-run the subgroup fairness and calibration checks before
promotion to `Production`.

## Limitations

Synthetic data only; not validated clinically; no causal interpretation. SHAP
attributions are explanatory aids, not clinical justifications.
